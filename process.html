My product design process

01Collect the dots

I always start by deconstructing problems into their fundamental elements, rooted in first principles. What problem are we addressing? Why is it important to solve? What pain points do our users face, and what are their specific use cases? What does the current market landscape reveal? What assumptions are we operating under, and what hypotheses do we hold?

During this phase, I gather insights from desk research, data analysis, competitor assessments, and stakeholder interviews. This information helps me understand user behaviors and sparks ideas for potential solutions.

02Connect the dots

After re-examining the problem or opportunity at its core, I leverage insights from the previous phase to craft the product strategy, outline the scope of work, understand our system limitations and dependencies, estimate required resources and time, and define success metrics. Much of this involves collaboration with my product and engineering counterparts.

With a bias for action, I prioritize creating tangible artifacts to visualize the team's ideas over lengthy documents or walls of text that often get overlooked. This hands-on approach not only accelerates decision-making but also fosters clearer communication and understanding among stakeholders. I delve into exploring design solutions through user flows and interactive prototypes, establishing design principles, and documenting key decisions. Sharing these design solutions and rationale with key stakeholders early on is essential to ensure alignment across teams and gather valuable feedback.

03Correct the dots

Next, I engage in peer reviews, stakeholder discussions, and technical feasibility assessments. High-fidelity mockups and rapid iterations are central to refining and enhancing our vision.

Once my team and I converge on a solution we believe to be optimal given the available information, I collaborate closely with engineering and QA teams throughout implementation and testing phases to ensure the accuracy of content and precise implementation of designs.

04Connect the dots, again

I firmly believe that design is an ongoing process and has no definitive finish line. Even as we continually tackle new initiatives, my team and I still make room to prioritize incremental improvements to the product following the launch of a new feature. We actively rely on data, insights, and feedback from users and stakeholders to guide our efforts. By leveraging user feedback and usage data, we pinpoint areas of the product ripe for enhancement and optimization, aiming to make it faster, more efficient, and user-friendly.
